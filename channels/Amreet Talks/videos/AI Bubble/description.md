Every month, millions of people pay $20 for ChatGPT.

But when I started doing the math on what AI actually costs to run, the numbers stopped adding up.

Reports suggest some AI companies are burning tens of billions of dollars a year. Chinese models just slashed their prices by over 90%. And AI data centers are slamming into a problem money can't fix: electricity.

So here's my theory on what's really going on — and what happens to your AI tools when the subsidy stops.

In this video I break down:
• The subsidy math — why running Claude Opus for a power user costs ~$60/month while you pay $20
• Who's quietly covering that gap right now (Microsoft, Amazon) and why
• How Chinese labs like DeepSeek blew up the pricing structure — an ~89x gap on output tokens
• OpenAI's reported burn rate — and why it's a different problem than Amazon's early losses
• Why this isn't really "dot-com 2.0" (the numbers don't match)
• The infrastructure wall nobody talks about — the power grid
• Which AI companies I think survive, and which don't

This isn't a news report. It's my theory — verified facts where I can verify them, clearly labeled speculation where I can't. The pricing, the Chinese price war, and the data-center bottlenecks are all public and checkable. The leaked financial projections are not, so I treat them as "if these numbers are even half right."

I could be wrong. But if these trends continue, the AI industry in three years could look very different from today.

What do you think happens when someone stops subsidizing your $20/month? Drop it in the comments.

📊 The numbers (all public, all checkable)
• Claude Opus 4.8 API: $5 in / $25 out per million tokens
• GPT-5.2 API: $1.75 in / $14 out per million tokens
• DeepSeek V4 Flash: $0.14 in / $0.28 out — ~89x cheaper on output
• DeepSeek cut its own API prices by 97.5% in a single announcement
• Google cut its AI Plus plan from $7.99 → $4.99/month (June 2026)
• ~50% of planned US data center builds delayed or canceled in 2026
• AI data center power draw: 100–300 MW continuous vs. 10–50 MW for a normal one
• Dot-com NASDAQ P/E peak: ~200 | AI 2026: ~30
