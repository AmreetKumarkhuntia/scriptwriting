## Hook

> "For the week of March 30th to April 5th, 2026, the six most-used AI models in the world — ranked by actual token consumption — were all made in China. Not OpenAI. Not Anthropic. Not Google. All six. And the #1 model by volume, MiniMax M2.5, costs $0.30 per million tokens. GPT-5.5 — OpenAI's current best — costs $5 per million tokens: 17x more expensive, for a model that scores just 8 percentage points higher on real-world coding benchmarks. And MiniMax isn't even the cheapest Chinese option. Step 3.5 Flash costs $0.10 per million tokens. That's 50x cheaper than GPT-5.5."

## Body Outline — 5 Chained Points (Ascending Shock Value)

**Point 1 — WHAT: The numbers that prove it already happened**
Cover the 61% OpenRouter share, all-top-6 week, benchmark parity at 5–18x lower cost. Goal: establish that this is real and measurable, not hype.
*Re-hook:* "But this wasn't one lab having a breakthrough. It was 8 labs, same window, same strategy. Who are they?"

**Point 2 — WHO: Eight labs, one playbook**
Quick tour of DeepSeek, Alibaba, Moonshot, Zhipu, MiniMax, ByteDance, StepFun, Baidu — origin, specialty, backer. Goal: make the ecosystem feel real and serious, not a list of names.
*Re-hook:* "Every one of them went open-source. They're giving away models that cost hundreds of millions to build. Why?"

**Point 3 — WHY: The open-source strategy isn't charity**
The flywheel: release open weights → 113K+ global derivatives → free eval data and fine-tuning returns to the lab. Western labs pay for red-teaming. Chinese labs outsource it to the world. Goal: this is where the viewer's mental model breaks — open-source as compounding leverage, not altruism.
*Re-hook:* "Okay, clever strategy. But has it actually worked? Is this real adoption or just developer hype?"

**Point 4 — HOW: The shift is real, measurable, and accelerating**
OpenRouter data: 61% consumption, 12.7x YoY growth, programming now >50% of all AI usage (was 11% in Jan 2025). Agentic coding workloads driving the switch — the use cases where volume makes price decisive. Goal: make the viewer feel the scale of the shift.
*Re-hook:* "For a startup or engineer, this all sounds like great news. Cheap, powerful, open models. But there's something nobody's talking about — and it's the part that should actually alarm you."

**Point 5 — THE ALARM: The open-source trap**
When Qwen, GLM, Kimi become the default foundation the world builds on, the defaults, alignment, and future versions are controlled by Chinese labs under Chinese law. 113K+ derivatives already. Every product built on top inherits those assumptions. The Huawei 5G debate at the model layer — already inside the global dev stack. Goal: land the thesis with alarm, not panic. The point isn't to stop using Chinese AI — it's to understand what you're building on.

- Close the loop on the hook: "That week in April wasn't an anomaly. It was a signal."
- High note: "The question isn't whether to use these models — plenty of engineers already are. The question is whether you know what you're building on."
- Optional CTA: "If you want to go deeper on any of these models, I've linked the full benchmark and pricing data in the description."
