# Research: AI Jobs Fear

## The Fear — What Was Predicted

- **Goldman Sachs (2023)**: AI could displace 300 million full-time jobs globally
- **McKinsey (2023)**: Up to 30% of work hours could be automated by 2030
- **OpenAI/Penn State study (2023)**: ~80% of US workers have at least 10% of their tasks exposed to GPT
- These predictions created widespread panic; media ran "AI will replace your job" headlines constantly

## The Actual 2023–2026 Data

- **Yale Budget Lab + Brookings Institution (2025)**: Found "no discernible disruption" to employment since ChatGPT launched (Nov 2022)
  - Source: https://fortune.com/2025/10/02/ai-job-losses-apocalypse-research-yale/
  - Source: https://www.brookings.edu/articles/new-data-show-no-ai-jobs-apocalypse-for-now/

- **WEF Future of Jobs Report (2025)**: Surveyed 1,000+ employers / 14M workers across 55 economies
  - 92 million jobs displaced by 2030
  - 170 million new jobs created by 2030
  - **Net: +78 million jobs**

- **February 2026**: AI cited in only 10% of job cuts — no broad layoff wave
  - Source: https://hub.jhu.edu/2026/02/23/will-ai-make-human-workers-obsolete/

- **PwC Global AI Jobs Barometer (2024)**: Industries with higher AI exposure saw:
  - +3.9% job growth
  - +4.8% wage growth
  - 4.8x higher productivity growth
  - Source: https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/services/ai/ai-jobs-barometer.html

- **Dallas Federal Reserve (2025)**: Economist consensus — AI won't majorly impact jobs "in the next decade"
  - Source: https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2025/0603

- **MIT CSAIL Study (2026)**: AI isn't "taking" jobs — gradually transforming how work gets done
  - Source: https://www.axios.com/2026/04/02/ai-jobs-mit-study-workforce-impact

## The Lump of Labor Fallacy

- **Named by David Schloss in 1891**: The false assumption that there is a fixed amount of work in the economy
- Core logic error: If AI does X task cheaper → goods/services cost less → people have more money → they spend it elsewhere → new industries and new jobs emerge
- This is how every prior tech revolution played out
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lump_of_labour_fallacy
- Source: https://www.ben-evans.com/benedictevans/2023/7/2/working-with-ai

**Important counterpoint to address**: Some economists argue AI could be *different* because it might eventually eliminate all comparative human advantage — not just tasks, but entire domains. Don't dismiss this. Acknowledge it, but note: this is a hypothetical future, not the present data.

## Historical Analogies

### 1. Luddite Rebellion (1811–1816)
- ~50,000 textile workers rioted against power looms and spinning frames
- Fear: "machines will take all our jobs"
- Reality: Real wages eventually caught up; industrial economy created far more jobs
- Source: https://www.weforum.org/stories/2020/09/short-history-jobs-automation/

### 2. Industrial Revolution (Late 1800s)
- Automation coincided with the largest expansion of employment in human history
- Middle-skilled jobs were "hollowed out" but total employment grew massively
- Source: https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/future-of-work/five-lessons-from-history-on-ai-automation-and-employment

### 3. Internet Revolution (1990–2000)
- Tech sector employment: +36% in 10 years
- Average weekly wages for tech workers: +102% in 10 years
- Entirely new job categories created: web designer, SEO specialist, network admin, social media manager — none existed in 1990
- Source: https://www.stlouisfed.org/publications/regional-economist/second-quarter-2017/growth-in-tech-sector-returns-to-glory-days-of-the-1990s

## David Autor — The Key Academic Voice

- MIT labor economist; considered one of the greatest in the field
- His thesis: Computers in the 1980s–2010s hollowed out the middle class (routine middle-skill jobs automated). AI could *reverse* this.
- AI can augment workers in writing, research, documentation — making mid-skill workers more competitive
- Key quote direction: "AI can either augment labor (complement workers) or automate it (substitute). The evidence points toward augmentation."
- Source: https://www.npr.org/transcripts/1176516094
- Source: https://voxdev.org/topic/technology-innovation/david-autor-ai-and-future-work

## BLS (Bureau of Labor Statistics) Projections 2023–2033

- Software developers: +17.9% (way above 4% avg)
- Personal financial advisors: +17.1%
- Bank tellers: -15% (down 51,400) — but this decline predates AI
- Cashiers: -11% (353,100 jobs) — same, largely driven by self-checkout, not LLMs
- Source: https://www.bls.gov/opub/ted/2025/ai-impacts-in-bls-employment-projections.htm

## CNN / MIT (2026) Summary Finding

- Workers gain time for higher-value activities
- Source: https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/10/tech/ai-taking-jobs

### Companies That Fired People and Then Rehired Them

**Klarna:**
- CEO claimed AI chatbot = equivalent of 700 full-time customer service agents, handled 2.3M conversations in first month
- Froze hiring, cut workforce from 7,000 → 3,000
- 15 months later: admitted AI "destroyed the quality of their customer service," launched a rehiring drive

**Duolingo:**
- CEO declared "AI first," phased out contractors (April 2025)
- Backtracked: "Most important thing is you're doing your job as well as possible. If AI can't help with that, I'm not going to force it."

**Salesforce:**
- Cut 9,000 customer support roles citing AI agent efficiency
- Months later: announced hiring 1,000 new graduates

→ **Use in script (Section 4 nuance)**: These aren't stories about AI failing — they're stories about companies that moved too fast, learned the hard way, and course-corrected. The jobs came back.
