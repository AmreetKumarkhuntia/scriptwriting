## SETUP (0:30–0:52)

**[VISUALS]** OpenAI / Anthropic / Google logos in a boxing-ring bracket → the bracket dissolves, a government seal drops on top of all three.

For three years we've been told the AI race is company versus company. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google.

June 12th proved that's the wrong map.

The most powerful player in AI isn't a company. It's the government that can switch any of them off — and it just showed it will.

Here's how it happened, in 72 hours.

---

## BEAT 1 — 72 HOURS FROM LAUNCH TO LIGHTS-OUT (0:52–1:25)

**[VISUALS]** Timeline: Jun 9 launch → Jun 12, 5:21 PM letter → screens across a world map blink off, one by one.

June 9th, Anthropic ships Claude Fable 5 — the most powerful model they'd ever put in public hands.

June 12th, a Friday, 5:21 PM: a letter hits Dario Amodei's desk from the Commerce Secretary.

Within hours, Fable 5 and its bigger sibling Mythos 5 go dark. Not throttled. Not region-locked. Off — for every person on Earth.

Seventy-two hours online. Then a government switched it off. First time in history that's ever happened to a live frontier AI.

Your first guess is "safety bug." And kind of. But *who* found that bug is where this gets uncomfortable.

---

## BEAT 2 — THE CALL CAME FROM INSIDE THE HOUSE (1:25–2:00)

**[VISUALS]** Amazon logo → arrow to the White House → "per Fortune" / "David Sacks" attribution chips pop in.

A "highly credible trusted partner" demonstrated a jailbreak — got the model to cough up restricted cyberattack information.

Reporting from Fortune says that partner was Amazon. And Amazon isn't a rival — it's one of Anthropic's biggest investors.

Their CEO reportedly took it straight to the White House. The ask, per David Sacks: patch it, or pull it. Anthropic refused — its own review called the flaw minor, and reproducible on public models like GPT-5.5.

So the government pulled it for them. And to do that, they reached for a law from 2018 that was never meant for this.

---

## BEAT 3 — AN EXPORT LAW BECAME A GLOBAL OFF-SWITCH (2:00–2:32)

**[VISUALS]** "Export Control Reform Act — 2018" → a missile icon + a chip icon → morph into a chat bubble → passport stamped "DENIED" over a spinning globe.

The Export Control Reform Act — the rules built to stop America shipping missile tech and chips abroad.

Commerce's export arm pointed it at a chatbot.

The order: no access for any foreign national — anywhere. But Anthropic can't sort its users by passport. So the only way to comply was to shut the model off for the entire planet.

A US export rule written for hardware just remotely switched off software running on everyone's screen.

Which leaves the question that actually matters: once a switch like that exists — who's holding it?

---

## BEAT 4 — IT'S NOT ANTHROPIC ANYMORE (2:32–3:10)

**[VISUALS]** A license stamp gating Fable's return → founders boarding a DC-bound flight → Palantir logo with a "$1B+ contracts" tag being ripped out → Anthropic's "please regulate us" quote bending into a lever.

Here's the quiet bombshell. Fable 5 didn't come back when Anthropic decided. It came back when the government granted a *license*.

They flew their co-founder and policy chief to Washington to negotiate the return of their own product.

Meanwhile Palantir — running Claude on over a billion dollars of defense contracts — had to rip it out and rebuild.

And sit with the irony. Anthropic built its whole brand on "please regulate us, AI is too dangerous." That exact argument became the lever that took its model away. <!-- optional trim: next line --> Its safety pitch didn't protect it — it armed the people who switched it off.

But then — how did that switch flip back on? The answer *is* the whole story.

---

## BEAT 5 — 19 DAYS LATER: FABLE COMES BACK (3:10–3:42)

**[VISUALS]** "Jul 1 — BACK ONLINE" → a second letter → the switch flips OFF then ON, both hands government-cuffed → Mythos 5 tagged "US ORGS ONLY".

Nineteen days. That's how long Fable 5 stayed gone.

Then, on July 1st, it came back.

But not because Anthropic won. Because Commerce sent *another* letter — once Anthropic patched the exact jailbreak with a new classifier that blocks it 99%+ of the time, and agreed to the government's terms.

And Mythos 5? It's still only open to approved US organizations.

So sit with this: the switch went *off* by Washington's hand — and back *on* by Washington's hand. Both times. Not the lab's.

Which means this was never a one-time Anthropic story. Because two weeks later, the target changed.

---

## BEAT 6 — NOW IT'S OPENAI'S TURN (3:42–4:14)

**[VISUALS]** OpenAI logo → White House → "GPT-5.6: Sol / Terra / Luna" → a "trusted partners only" gate → "per CNN / CNBC" chip.

Around June 25th, the White House went to OpenAI.

The ask: limit the release of your new models — GPT-5.6, codenamed Sol, Terra, and Luna — to a small group of "trusted partners."

And here's the difference. With Anthropic, it was a legally binding order. With OpenAI, it was just a request.

But OpenAI didn't even wait to be ordered. They complied — voluntarily. (Per CNN and CNBC.)

Two different companies. Two weeks apart. One hand.

This stopped being an exception. It's now a pattern.

But there's still one kind of AI this switch can never reach.

---

## BEAT 7 — KICKER: YOU CAN'T SWITCH OFF WHAT'S ALREADY DOWNLOADED (4:14–4:42)

**[VISUALS]** Closed model = one server + one plug. Open weights = files copied onto a million hard drives, un-pluggable. Mistral + Chinese-lab logos glowing "OPEN".

A kill switch only works on a closed model. One company, one set of servers, one plug to pull.

It does nothing to open weights already sitting on a million hard drives.

Mistral's answer was basically: fine, we'll go open. And China's models already are.

So the lesson is brutal for America: closed US AI can be switched off by Washington. Open Chinese AI can't be switched off by anyone.

The ban meant to protect American AI dominance might be the best ad open-source has ever gotten.
