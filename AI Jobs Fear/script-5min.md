## HOOK (0:00–0:30)

Goldman Sachs published a report in 2023 saying AI could eliminate 300 million full-time jobs worldwide.

That was three years ago.

So — how many jobs has AI actually destroyed?

[PAUSE]

According to Yale University and the Brookings Institution — there has been zero discernible disruption to overall employment since ChatGPT launched.

Zero.

Today I'm going to show you why the panic is built on a 130-year-old logical mistake — and what the data actually says.

---

## THE FALLACY (0:30–2:15)

Here's the mistake everyone keeps making. Economists identified it in 1891. It even has a name.

In 1891, a British economist named David Schloss coined the "Lump of Labor Fallacy."

The idea: people assume there's a fixed amount of work in the economy. Like a pie. If a machine does some of it, there's less pie for humans.

But that's not how economies work.

Think about the power loom — one of the machines that triggered the Luddite Rebellion in 1811. Thousands of textile workers rioted across England. The British government deployed 12,000 troops to suppress them — more soldiers than Wellington had led into Portugal just three years earlier. They believed the machines would end their livelihoods.

Two hundred years later, real wages had doubled and employment had exploded. The jobs the Luddites couldn't imagine — factory managers, quality inspectors, railway workers — absorbed everyone the machines had "displaced."

This is the pattern every single time. Technology doesn't shrink the amount of work. It changes what the work is.

---

## THE DATA (2:15–3:30)

But you're thinking — AI is different. More powerful than the power loom.

Fair. So let's look at actual data from the last three years.

ChatGPT launched in November 2022. If AI was going to cause a jobs crisis, we'd be seeing it by now.

Here's what the data shows.

**One.** The World Economic Forum — over a thousand employers, 14 million workers, 55 economies. Their 2025 report: yes, AI will displace 92 million jobs by 2030. But it will create 170 million new ones. Net gain: 78 million jobs.

**Two.** Industries with higher AI exposure are actually growing jobs faster than average. In 2024, AI-exposed sectors saw 3.9% job growth and 4.8% wage growth. More AI exposure — better outcomes. The opposite of what the panic predicts.

**Three.** Through early 2026, AI was cited as the reason in roughly 8 to 10% of all job cuts. The other 90%? Economic slowdowns. Market conditions. Strategic pivots. The usual.

Three years in, the data doesn't support the panic.

---

## THE FLIP (3:30–4:30)

Here's the angle no one talks about.

The jobs AI genuinely cannot do — the ones with the highest resilience — are overwhelmingly blue-collar and physical.

Electricians, emergency responders, plumbers, HVAC technicians, surgeons, nurses — consistently ranked among the least automatable jobs across every major study. These jobs require physical work in unpredictable environments, split-second judgment, and human trust.

AI cannot send a robot into your house to fix your wiring. Not yet. Not for a long time.

The narrative we've been sold: AI threatens low-skill workers. The data says the opposite. The jobs most at risk are white-collar, knowledge-based, routine cognitive work. The trades are the safest.

---

## OUTRO (4:30–5:00)

Will AI change your job? Almost certainly yes.

Will it eliminate your job? The data, the history, and the economics all say: probably not.

The real risk isn't AI taking your job. It's someone who knows how to use AI taking your job.

This was never AI versus humans. It's humans with AI versus humans without it.
