# YouTube Scriptwriting Framework

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7I50PECz7SU

---

## Core Psychology: Expectations vs. Reality

This is the master principle behind everything below.

- **Expectations** = what the viewer thinks is going to happen
- **Reality** = what actually happens

When reality beats expectations → viewer is happy, keeps watching, you win.
When expectations beat reality → viewer is disappointed, stops watching, you lose.

Every decision in your script is about managing and beating this gap. Keep this lens on at all times.

---

## The 5-Step Framework

Write scripts in this exact order: **Packaging → Outline → Intro → Body → Outro**

---

### Step 1 — Packaging

Lock these three things *before* writing a single word of script.

**Idea**
One sentence describing the video. This is either:
- The pain point you're solving for your target viewer, or
- The rabbit hole you want to pull them into

If the idea isn't something people would actually want to watch, no script will save it.

**Title** *(must be finalized before writing the intro)*
The title sets the incoming expectation you'll have to beat. Ask yourself:
- Does this trigger a **click curiosity loop** — is the viewer curious enough about the pain/problem that they can't resist clicking?
- What exact expectation does a viewer have after reading this title? (Slight wording changes shift this significantly.)

Tools for optimizing titles: vidIQ, Spotter Studio, TubeBuddy, VidStats.

**Thumbnail**
A loose idea is fine. Don't let thumbnail perfectionism block you from writing — that back-and-forth kills momentum. Dial it in over time.

---

### Step 2 — Outline

Write a bulleted list of body points **before** writing the intro. This is "measure twice, cut once."

**Why outline first?**
The intro is learnable — it follows a formula. The real differentiator is whether you have something *genuinely worth saying* in the body. Gut-check that first.

**The novelty test**
For each bullet ask: is this unique, or am I just summarizing what everyone already knows?

- If your points are rehashing existing base knowledge, you can't beat expectations — the viewer already knows this stuff
- If the outline doesn't pass the novelty test, **stop and do more research** before writing

**For each bullet, layer on three things:**

| Layer | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **What** | What is this point, as simply as possible? |
| **Why** | Why does it matter? |
| **How** | How does it fit into the overall story of the video? |

---

### Step 3 — Intro

Goal: confirm the click *and* open a curiosity loop the viewer can't help but go down.

Use this 4-part framework in order:

**Part 1 — Click Confirmation**
Bluntly state what the video is about in the first few lines. The viewer needs to immediately confirm that clicking was worth it. This directly manages expectations vs. reality at first contact.

> Example: *"Today we're talking about writing killer scripts. The reason winning creators go viral so often is because of their scriptwriting..."*

**Part 2 — Common Belief**
Restate the conventional take on the topic — what your viewer already believes. This does two things:
- Creates common ground ("this person gets my problem")
- Sets up the contrast you're about to deliver

> Example: *"This is an art, and it's one of the hardest things to learn when you're starting out making videos."*

**Part 3 — Contrarian Contrast**
Challenge the common belief with a different approach. This is where you first beat their expectations — you're offering a perspective they haven't heard before.

> Example: *"But the good news is there is a right answer. I've analyzed hundreds of creators across every niche and they all run the exact same scriptwriting process."*

**Part 4 — Proof + Plan** *(can be in either order)*
- **Proof**: Why should they trust your new approach? Credentials, track record, evidence.
- **Plan**: An ordered list of what's coming in the video (sets the roadmap and compounds curiosity).

> Example: *"This is the proven 5-step framework for how to write killer scripts that keep viewers hooked. By the way, I'm Callaway, I've done over a billion views..."*

By the end of the intro, the viewer should be Hook, Line, and Sinker.

---

### Step 4 — Body

**The Value Loop** — use this structure for every single point:

| Stage | What to do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Context** | Say what the point is. Clear, concise, digestible — surface level first. |
| **Application** | Say how to do it. Use relevant examples to make it actionable. |
| **Framing** | Say why this point matters and how it connects to the whole video's story. |

This mirrors how the brain processes information: base facts first, then inference, then connection.

---

**Point ordering — ascending shock value**

Counter-intuitively, do *not* lead with your best point. Order them like this:

1. 2nd-best point
2. Best point
3. 3rd-best point
4. ...and so on

**Why:** Human brains detect patterns. If point 2 is better than point 1, the viewer subconsciously expects point 3 to be even better — and they stay. If your best comes first and point 2 is slightly weaker, the pattern signals diminishing value and they leave.

This is why music albums rarely put the hit single first — it's usually track 3 or 4, after two strong but slightly lesser tracks have built the pattern.

---

**Re-hooking between points**

At the end of each body point, open a mini curiosity loop before transitioning to the next one. Attention naturally decays after a peak — re-hooking resets it.

> Example: *"That point was super important, but if you don't also couple it with this next one, the magic is lost."*

---

**Zoom in / zoom out rhythm**

Alternate between big picture (why it matters, the overall story) and tactical (the specific technique). This wave pattern aids comprehension and keeps viewers oriented in the larger narrative while still extracting actionable takeaways.

---

### Step 5 — Outro

The outro is not an afterthought. This is where likes, comments, shares, and rewatches come from.

- Summarize the key points from the video
- Remind the viewer of the pain point you solved — close the loop
- Final reinforcement that reality beat their expectations ("we actually did what I promised")
- Leave them on a **high note** — the last impression drives all the algorithm-positive actions

Think of it like the free chocolate a restaurant gives you with the check. It's intentional psychology — ending on a positive moment makes the whole experience feel better than it was.

---

## Bonus: Native Embeds (CTAs Without Killing Flow)

The goal is to include a call-to-action without it feeling like an ad break.

**How to do it:**
1. During the outline phase, identify which body point naturally connects to a resource or offer you have
2. At that point in the script: frame the pain → give your resource as the solve
3. Never stop the flow to insert a CTA — it should feel like a natural extension of the point

**Two formats that work:**
- **Open loop → solve with resource**: raise the pain, then offer the resource as the answer
- **Speedrun → offer to recap**: go through a framework so fast they can't fully absorb it, then offer the resource as a way to review or accelerate implementation

## Quick-Reference Checklist

### Before writing
- [ ] Idea is a clear pain point or rabbit hole (one sentence)
- [ ] Title is finalized — click curiosity loop passes the gut check
- [ ] Outline is written and passes the novelty test
- [ ] Every outline point has What / Why / How layered on

### Intro
- [ ] Click confirmation in the first 2–3 lines
- [ ] Common belief stated
- [ ] Contrarian contrast delivered
- [ ] Proof and plan both included

### Body
- [ ] Every point uses Context → Application → Framing
- [ ] Points ordered by ascending shock value (2nd-best first, best second)
- [ ] Re-hook written between each point
- [ ] Zoom in / zoom out rhythm maintained

### Outro
- [ ] Key points summarized
- [ ] Pain→solve arc reinforced
- [ ] Ends on a high note

### CTA (if applicable)
- [ ] CTA is natively embedded — not a flow break
- [ ] Pain framed → resource offered as the solve
