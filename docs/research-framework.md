# YouTube Research & Scripting Framework

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q2bOMglypxg

---

## Step 1: Generate Research Points — The Kipling Method (5W1H)

Before you start Googling anything, apply these 6 questions to your topic:

| Question | What to cover |
|----------|---------------|
| **What** | Define it. What exactly is it? How does it differ from related things? |
| **Who** | People involved — creators, victims, key figures, companies |
| **When** | Timeline, origin, historical events, when it became popular |
| **Where** | Location, geography, where it's most relevant |
| **Why** | Motivations, intentions, root causes, why it matters |
| **How** | Mechanisms, technical aspects, processes, impact |

> Not every question will be equally important for every topic. A video about WWII needs heavy "Where" coverage. A video about MrBeast's rise doesn't.

**Stuck? Use this ChatGPT prompt:**
> "I'm making a YouTube video on the topic [XYZ]. How can I apply the 5W1H method to research for this video?"

## Step 2: Research & Verify Sources

### Where to research
- Google (articles, news)
- Wikipedia (overview + linked sources)
- YouTube (documentaries, interviews, talks)
- Books

### AI tools for research
- **Perplexity AI** — shows citations alongside answers; lets you verify sources and dig deeper
- Avoid using raw ChatGPT for facts — it doesn't show sources and can hallucinate

### Academic / scientific topics
- **Google Scholar** — academic papers and studies
- **Consensus** — AI search engine that answers questions based on scientific evidence

### Source credibility checklist
- [ ] Is the website trustworthy?
- [ ] Is the author credible?
- [ ] Avoid Quora/Reddit as primary sources (likely biased)
- [ ] Extra caution for: medicine, science, finance, politics, controversial subjects

## Step 3: Go Deep — Horizontal & Vertical Logic

### Horizontal Logic
List multiple angles on the same level.

**Example — "Why do people smoke?"**
- Peer pressure
- Stress relief
- Advertising

### Vertical Logic
Drill down into a single angle to find the root cause.

**Example — drilling into "peer pressure":**
1. Why do people face peer pressure?
   → They want to fit into their social circle
2. Why do they want to fit in?
   → Belonging is a basic human need
3. Why is it a basic need?
   → Historically, being part of a tribe meant protection, resources, reproduction

Use both types for every major question in your research. That's what makes a video genuinely informative vs. surface-level.

## Step 4: Write the Script

### 4a. The Intro (Hook)
No single formula — pick what fits your topic:

| Hook type | How it works | Best for |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Shocking stat/data** | Opens with a striking number | Business, social issues |
| **Story** | Tells a short story that raises questions | History, biography |
| **Teaser clip** | Shows the most attention-grabbing moment upfront | Drama, controversy |

The common thread: make viewers *curious* enough to keep watching.

### 4b. Main Content
1. Answer basic questions first (What, When, Who)
2. Then move to the more interesting/surprising ones
3. Only go deep on questions *relevant* to your video's core topic

   **Example:** In a Mona Lisa video:
   - Don't rabbit-hole into Leonardo Da Vinci's full biography
   - DO go deep on "Who is the woman in the painting?" — that's what viewers came for

### 4c. Transitions & Retention
Keep viewers watching by linking points together:
- End one point by raising a question that the *next* point answers
- Mention something that contradicts the current point — then resolve it
- The video should feel like a movie, not a bullet-point list

## Quick Reference Checklist

### Research phase
- [ ] Applied 5W1H to generate all relevant questions
- [ ] Found answers via articles, Wikipedia, YouTube, books
- [ ] Verified sources for credibility
- [ ] Used horizontal logic to cover multiple angles
- [ ] Used vertical logic to find root causes on key questions

### Script phase
- [ ] Hook written (stat / story / teaser)
- [ ] Basic questions answered first
- [ ] Deep dives only on relevant questions
- [ ] Points linked with transitions, not abrupt cuts
- [ ] Ending CTA written
