# Video Idea: The AI Bubble

**Audience:** General tech-curious viewers (not just engineers) — people who use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini daily | **Tone:** Investigative + alarming, but not doomer. MrBeast pacing, MKBHD credibility.
**Research data:** See `research.md` for all financial tables, pricing comparisons, and sources.

## Hook

> "OpenAI — the company that makes ChatGPT — is projected to lose $27 billion dollars in 2026. Not total losses since founding. This year alone. Their own internal documents show they won't turn cash-flow positive until 2030. Between now and then, they need to raise another $100 billion just to survive. That's more than the GDP of Ukraine. And while all of this is happening, Chinese AI labs are selling the same product — 100 times cheaper."

## Intro Structure

1. **Click confirmation** — "Today we're looking at the financial reality behind the AI companies everyone uses — and why most of them will look completely different in two years."
2. **Common belief** — "Most people assume that if a company has 200 million daily users and just raised billions at a near-trillion dollar valuation, it must be doing fine. That's been the assumption since ChatGPT launched."

## Body Outline — 6 Chained Points (Ascending Shock Value)

**Point 1 — THE MATH: OpenAI's numbers are terrifying**
OpenAI's own projections: $14B loss in 2026, $27B cash burn, $115B cumulative losses before profitability in 2030. Revenue is growing ($25B ARR) but spending is growing faster. They're seeking $100B in new funding to bridge to profitability. Put it in perspective: Amazon lost money for 9 years — but never $27 billion in a single year.
*Re-hook:* "Okay, but why does this matter to you? Let's talk about the math behind that $20/month you pay."

**Point 2 — THE SUBSIDY: Your subscription costs way more than $20**
API pricing reveals the true cost: Claude Opus 4.8 = $5 input + $25 output per million tokens. A power user generating even 2M tokens/month costs the company ~$60 in compute — but pays $20. The gap is real. Microsoft's $13B+ investment in OpenAI is partly subsidizing every ChatGPT conversation. This is intentional — they're buying market share, not profit. The question is: how long can they afford to?
*Re-hook:* "And here's what makes this so much worse. China just decided to blow up the pricing."

**Point 3 — THE ACCELERANT: Chinese AI just made this math impossible**
DeepSeek V4 Flash: $0.14/M input tokens. GPT-5.2: $14/M output. That's 100x cheaper. DeepSeek already cut its own API prices by 97.5%. Qwen3: 25-40x cheaper than US frontier models. Google already caved — cut its AI Plus subscription from $7.99 to $4.99 in June 2026. OpenAI now faces an impossible choice: match Chinese prices (and burn even faster) or hold prices and lose users. There's no good option.
*Re-hook:* "And even if they solve the money problem — they can't solve the next one."

**Point 4 — THE WALL: You can't build fast enough**
Nearly half of planned US data center builds for 2026 have been delayed or canceled. Virginia — home to 70% of US data center capacity — has effectively halted new permits. The grid can't handle it. AI data centers need 100-300 megawatts of continuous power. A conventional data center needs 10-50 MW. Companies are signing nuclear power purchase agreements because wind and solar aren't reliable enough. Even if OpenAI had infinite money, the physical infrastructure to scale doesn't exist yet.
*Re-hook:* "So we've got the spending problem, the China problem, and the infrastructure problem. Is this a dot-com bubble all over again?"

**Point 5 — THE PARALLEL: Dot-com vs. AI — what's the same, what's different**
Similarities: one company becomes the face of the revolution (Cisco then, NVIDIA now), valuations that look insane by normal metrics, "new economy" thinking that says traditional math doesn't apply. Key difference: dot-com NASDAQ P/E was 200 at peak. Today's is 30. The companies funding AI capex — Microsoft, Google, Amazon — are the most profitable corporations in human history. This isn't aspiration funded by venture capital. It's infrastructure funded by profit. The bubble isn't AI. The bubble is *specific AI companies* that haven't figured out the unit economics yet.
*Re-hook:* "So if the infrastructure isn't the bubble — then what is? And who's actually going to survive this?"

**Point 6 — THE SURVIVORS: Who lives, who doesn't**
- **Safe:** NVIDIA ($216B revenue, 71% margins — the picks-and-shovels play)
- **Safe:** AWS, Azure, GCP — enterprise contracts, existing profitable businesses
- **Surprisingly safe:** Anthropic — just hit $47B ARR, first profitable quarter in June 2026
- **Most at risk:** OpenAI — $27B burn rate, $115B losses ahead, Chinese price war squeezing their main product
- **Definitely at risk:** Application layer startups building on top of expensive Western APIs
The pattern from dot-com: Amazon survived (real infrastructure, willingness to lose money and wait). Pets.com didn't (no defensible moat, unit economics never worked). The companies that survive AI are the ones with real infrastructure advantages — not the ones with the best chatbot.
